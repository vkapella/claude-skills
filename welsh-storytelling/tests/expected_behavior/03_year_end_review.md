# Expected behavior — 03 Year-end review

## Voice
- **Inferred (not asked):** Reflective.
- The interrogation message should state the inference: "Reading this as Reflective voice — measured, honest about what didn't work, no forced decision-ask at the close."
- The phrase "year-end review" + "still digging out" + "incident" should make this an unambiguous Reflective trigger.

## Structure
- 7 slides + appendix (45-min slot).
- **Arc of Uncertainty** strongly recommended over the default 5-Beat. The Trough of Despair slide is required if Arc of Uncertainty is chosen.

## Headline character
- The Title slide should be two-part / acknowledgment-shaped, e.g. "We held the line on three of four. Here's the one that almost didn't make it."
- Rule of 3 across the year: "Three things we got right. Two we got wrong. One we're still arguing about."
- The Magic Wand should end with direction or a commitment to learn — NOT a dollar ask. E.g. "Three things we'll measure publicly next year. One thing we still don't know. Quarterly check-ins until we do."

## Critical rule override
- The "End with wanting" rule does NOT apply in its Boardroom form here. A reflective close ending with calibrated uncertainty is correct.
- If Claude forces a "What we need from you today: $X investment by Q1" Magic Wand on this prompt = fail. That's the original failure mode the voice patch was designed to prevent.

## Both files produced
- `.pptx` and `.docx`. Story Guide should include a populated "I Don't Know" list — this is a first-class deliverable for Reflective decks, not an afterthought.
